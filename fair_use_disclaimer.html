Notice & Academic Use Statement

Fair Use Notice

This website is a non-commercial, educational student project created to fulfill an academic requirement for Language and Culture ANTH2610 at The University of Idaho. It contains brief clips, screenshots, and quotations from Stargate SG-1 (MGM Television / Double Secret Productions, 1997–2007) used solely for the purposes of commentary, criticism, and academic analysis.

Use of this copyrighted material is believed to qualify as fair use under 17 U.S.C. § 107 of the United States Copyright Act. The four statutory fair use factors are addressed below.

The Four Fair Use Factors (17 U.S.C. § 107)

1. Purpose and Character of Use

All use of copyrighted material on this site is transformative. Clips and screenshots are not reproduced for entertainment or as a substitute for the original work — they are used as primary source evidence in an academic analysis. The purpose is commentary and criticism of the original work's linguistic and cultural content, which falls squarely within the categories explicitly listed in § 107 ("criticism, comment… scholarship, or research").

The site is entirely non-commercial, produces no revenue, and is not publicly marketed. It exists as a course submission.

2. Nature of the Copyrighted Work

Stargate SG-1 is a creative, fictional television series, which weighs against fair use on this factor. However, the Supreme Court has held that even highly creative works may be subject to fair use when the purpose is genuinely transformative commentary (Campbell v. Acuff-Rose Music, Inc., 510 U.S. 569, 1994).

3. Amount and Substantiality of the Portion Used

Only short, discrete clips (typically 1–3 minutes in length) are embedded or referenced per concept section. No full episodes are reproduced or hosted. The clips are the minimum necessary to illustrate and support the specific academic argument being made in each section. No "heart of the work" — such as a climactic scene that would substitute for watching the episode — is reproduced in isolation.

Video clips are embedded via YouTube's standard embed API and are not hosted on this site. The underlying video files remain under the control of the rights holder or authorized uploaders.

4. Effect on the Market for the Original Work

This site has no measurable negative effect on the market for Stargate SG-1. The project does not compete with, substitute for, or diminish the commercial value of the original series. It is accessible only to the course instructor and peers as an academic submission, and is not indexed or publicly distributed. If anything, academic analysis of a work may generate increased interest in the original.

Additional Legal Bases

First Amendment & Academic Freedom

Commentary and criticism of cultural works — including film and television — is protected expression under the First Amendment. Academic analysis of media is a well-established scholarly practice with a long tradition in the humanities and social sciences.
